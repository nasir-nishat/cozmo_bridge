5/15/26, 10:02 PM - COZE_Ricky: 🏠🇰🇷 Korean Climate Comfort

Why “Cool” & “Warm” Live in Different Worlds — And Why AC Isn’t Used in Winter

Korean homes are designed with two completely separate systems: a fast cooling machine for summer and a gentle floor-heating system for winter. Because the outdoor air is extremely cold during winter, just opening a window instantly cools the room, so there’s no need to run air-conditioning at all.

Below is the full guide for guests to understand how everything works and how to stay comfortable year-round in Korea.


---

1. Two Totally Separate Systems

❄️ Room-by-Room Cooling (Summer A/C)

Each bedroom or living room has its own wall-mounted air-conditioner.

Controlled with an individual remote (look for the ❄️ snowflake).

Korea doesn’t use central HVAC ducts—so AC is local and quick.

In winter, AC is almost never used; the outside air is already freezing, so simply opening a window cools the space in seconds.


🔥 Ondol Radiant Floor Heating (Winter Heating)

A gas or electric boiler pumps warmth underneath the floor (called 온돌).

Heat rises slowly but stays warm for a long time—perfect for floor-focused living.

Controlled by a separate wall panel labeled 보일러 / 난방.

Ideal for cold seasons when cozy floors matter more than warm blowing air.


Think of them as two different appliances living in the same home:
A “cold-air blower” for hot, humid summers, and a “warm-floor maker” for Korea’s freezing winters.


---

2. Why AC Isn’t Used in Korean Winters ❄️➡️🪟

Korean winter temperatures often drop below freezing.
So instead of turning on AC:

Open a window → indoor temperature drops immediately.

Korean buildings are designed to retain heat, not to cool in winter.

All energy use focuses on heating, not cooling.

Running AC in winter is unnecessary and uncommon.


Most locals simply ventilate briefly, and the room becomes cool right away.


---

3. How This Differs from Western Homes

🎛️ Many Controls vs. One Thermostat

U.S./Europe: one thermostat for entire home.
Korea:

AC remote per room

One boiler panel for floor heat


🌬️ No Ducts → No Central HVAC

Western homes push hot/cold air through vents.
Korean homes rely on:

direct air from AC units

floor-based radiant heat for winter


⏱️ Different Speeds

AC cools quickly.

Ondol heats slowly (30–60 min) but stays warm for hours.


🧦 Different Feeling of Warmth

Western heat = warm air blowing.
Korean heat = warm floors that gently warm your body from the feet up.


---

4. Travel Tips for Using Korean Heating & Cooling

1. Find both controls first
AC remotes → under each indoor unit
Boiler panel → near entrance or kitchen


2. Start Ondol early
Turn it on 30–60 min before sleep.


3. Shut doors when cooling
Bathrooms/hallways rarely have AC.


4. Mix systems freely
It’s normal to have one room cooling while another uses floor heating—it won’t damage anything.


5. Take photos of panels
Brands differ; a photo helps your host guide you instantly.




---

5. Why Korea Uses This Split System

🏢 High-rise apartment design – slim water pipes fit better than big HVAC ducts
🧘‍♂️ Floor-centric lifestyle – warmth underfoot feels natural and luxurious
💸 Energy efficiency – cool only the rooms you use, heat only the floor zones you need
❄️ Free winter cooling – the outdoor air keeps homes cool without AC


---

✨ In Short

Korea’s climate control works like two friendly roommates:
One blasts cool air where you point, and the other wraps your feet in gentle warmth.
And in winter, nature itself becomes the cooling system—just crack open a window and the room refreshes instantly.

Stay cozy and comfortable in any season! 🧡

🧻 What goes in:
Small plastic wrappers, dirty packaging
Used tissues, cotton swabs, sanitary items
Chicken bones, large fish bones 🐔🐟
Rubber gloves, sponges, toothbrushes

🚫 Please DON’T take your trash outside yourself — Seoul’s system can be tricky. 

✅ DO THIS INSTEAD:

Use the indoor trash bins for non-recyclables
Separate this from recyclables

🛎️ Let our staff know when it’s full — we’ll collect it for you!

2️⃣ Food Waste (음식물 쓰레기 – Organic Waste)

🥕 What goes in:
Leftover rice, noodles, bread, fruits & veggies
Eggshells, coffee grounds ☕
Small fish bones

🧯 How to dispose:

Use the sink garbage disposer in the kitchen

Turn on the water 💧, drop in soft food scraps, and grind 

⚠️ DON’T put in hard or fibrous waste (like bones, shells, onion peels or corn husks) — they can break the system!

Chicken bones & large scraps → go in general waste

3️⃣ Recyclables (재활용 – Clean & Sorted Only!)

♻️ What can be recycled? 

Plastics 🧴
Bottles, takeout containers — rinse before disposal 

Glass 🍾
Wine bottles, jars — no food inside! 

Paper & Cardboard 📦
Newspapers, flattened boxes, clean cartons 

Cans & Metals 🥫
Soda & food cans — rinse thoroughly 

Styrofoam 🧊
Must be clean & dry

🗂️ How to Dispose:

🤢 If it's dirty or has food? → That’s general waste, not recycling!
🛎️ Full bins? Let us know, and we’ll take care of it.

4️⃣ Large Waste (대형 폐기물 – Bulky Items)

🧳 Includes:
Travel suitcases, furniture, mattresses, electronics

🚫 Dumping large items in public is not allowed and can result in a fine.
