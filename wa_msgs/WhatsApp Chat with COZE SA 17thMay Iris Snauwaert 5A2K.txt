Chicken bones, large fish bones 🐔🐟
Rubber gloves, sponges, toothbrushes
🚫 Please DON’T take your trash outside yourself — Seoul’s system can be tricky. ✅ DO THIS INSTEAD:
Use the indoor trash bins for non-recyclables
Separate this from recyclables
🛎️ Let our staff know when it’s full — we’ll collect it for you!

🥕 What goes in:
Leftover rice, noodles, bread, fruits & veggies
Eggshells, coffee grounds ☕
Small fish bones
🧯 How to dispose:
Use the sink garbage disposer in the kitchen
Turn on the water 💧, drop in soft food scraps, and grind ⚠️ DON’T put in hard or fibrous waste (like bones, shells, onion peels or corn husks) — they can break the system!
Chicken bones & large scraps → go in general waste

Bottles, takeout containers — rinse before disposal Glass 🍾
Wine bottles, jars — no food inside! Paper & Cardboard 📦
Newspapers, flattened boxes, clean cartons Cans & Metals 🥫
Soda & food cans — rinse thoroughly Styrofoam 🧊
Must be clean & dry
🗂️ How to Dispose:
🤢 If it's dirty or has food? → That’s general waste, not recycling!
🛎️ Full bins? Let us know, and we’ll take care of it.

4️⃣ Large Waste (대형 폐기물 – Bulky Items)
🧳 Includes:
Travel suitcases, furniture, mattresses, electronics

⚠️ Important Reminders
