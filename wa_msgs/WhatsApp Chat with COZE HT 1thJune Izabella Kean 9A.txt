About 110V outlet converters / voltage converters — here’s the detailed guide:

• Universal power strip in every bedroom 🧩🔌
Each room is equipped with a universal multi-outlet power strip, so most travelers can plug in their chargers easily.

• Do you need a “down converter”? ⚡️
In most cases, no. For modern electronics like:

phone chargers

laptop chargers

camera / power bank chargers

tablet chargers
these are usually dual-voltage (100–240V), so they work perfectly in Korea with the right plug fit.

✅ Tip: Please check your adapter label — if it says “Input: 100–240V”, you do NOT need a converter.

• Important: We do NOT provide a down converter
We don’t keep voltage converters at the home — because they’re rarely needed for most guests’ devices.

• When a converter might be necessary (rare)
If you’re bringing high-power or motor/heating appliances such as:

hair dryer / curler / straightener (some models)

electric shaver (older models)

travel iron

CPAP that is NOT dual-voltage
then a converter may be needed only if the device is 110V-only.

If you think you may need one, just tell us the device name + a quick photo of the power label (where voltage is written), and we’ll guide you exactly what to prepare 🙏✨

# 📋 User Guide: Seoul Climate Card (기후동행카드)
The **Climate Card** is a prepaid transit card that offers unlimited access to Seoul’s public transportation (subway, buses, and the Tashu/Ttareungi public bicycle system) for a fixed price during a specific period.
## 1. Where to Buy & Top Up
### 💳 Physical Card (Recommended for Foreigners)
 * **Where to Buy:** You can purchase the physical card for **3,000 KRW (Cash only)** at:
   * Customer Service Centers inside Seoul Subway Stations (Line 1–8).
   * Convenience stores near subway stations (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven, Emart24).
 * **Where to Top Up:** At the automated ticket vending machines located inside Seoul subway stations **(Cash only)**.
> ⚠️ **Note:** Mobile cards are currently only available for Android users with a domestic resident registration number/Korean credit card. Foreign tourists are highly recommended to use the **Physical Card**.
> 
## 2. Pass Types & Fares
You can select the duration that best fits your travel itinerary:

| Pass Type | Price (Without Bicycle) | Price (With Ttareungi Bicycle) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1-Day Pass** | 5,000 KRW | 5,500 KRW |
| **2-Day Pass** | 8,000 KRW | 8,500 KRW |
| **3-Day Pass** | 10,000 KRW | 10,500 KRW |
| **5-Day Pass** | 15,000 KRW | 15,500 KRW |
| **30-Day Pass** | 62,000 KRW | 65,000 KRW |

*The pass becomes valid immediately upon topping up at the machine.*
## 3. Coverage & Boundaries
### ✅ What is INCLUDED:
 * **Seoul Subway Lines:** All lines within the Seoul city boundary (Lines 1–9, Ui-Sinseol Line, Sillim Line, Gyeongui-Jungang Line, Gyeongchun Line, Suin-Bundang Line, and Airport Railroad **local trains**).
 * **Seoul City Buses:** Blue, Green, Maeul (local community), and Night (Late-night) buses.
### ❌ What is EXCLUDED:
 * **Shinbundang Line** (due to a different fare system).
 * **Express Buses, Intercity Buses, and Red Buses** (Gyeonggi-do/Incheon licensed buses entering Seoul).
 * **Airport Railroad Express Trains (AREX).**
 * *Getting off outside Seoul:* If you board within Seoul but get off at a station outside the designated network (e.g., deep into Gyeonggi-do), you must pay the extra fare to the station staff.
## 4. How to Use
### 🚇 On Subways & Buses
 1. **Tap ON:** Tap your card against the sensor on the turnstile or bus card reader when boarding.
 2. **Tap OFF (CRITICAL):** You **MUST tap your card again when exiting** the subway or bus.
   * *If you fail to tap off twice within a short period, your card will be suspended/locked for 24 hours.*
### 🚲 On Ttareungi (Seoul Public Bicycles)
*If you purchased a pass that includes bicycle access:*
 1. Download the **'Ttareungi (Seoul Bike)'** app.
 2. Register your Climate Card number in the app to issue a voucher.
 3. Use the app to scan the QR code on the bike to unlock it.
## 💡 Quick Tips for Visitors
 * **One Card Per Person:** Each traveler must have their own physical Climate Card; it cannot be shared or tapped multiple times for multiple people.
 * **Keep Cash Handy:** Both purchasing the physical card and topping it up at the station machines require **Korean Won cash**. Credit cards are not accepted at the vending machines for tourist passes.
