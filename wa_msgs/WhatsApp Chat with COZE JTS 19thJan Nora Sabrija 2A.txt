5/5/26, 8:45 PM - COZE_RICKY: Here’s how EMS shipping works from Korea to Germany, especially if you want to send a lamp.

EMS stands for Express Mail Service. It is Korea Post’s international express shipping service. You can send packages overseas through a Korean post office, and you can track the shipment with a tracking number.

For sending a lamp to Germany by EMS, it is usually possible if it is a regular plug-in table lamp. The safest type to send is:

A regular electric lamp
No battery inside
No bulb included
No oil, gas, or liquid
For personal use

Before shipping, it is best to remove the bulb and make sure there is no battery inside. Rechargeable lamps, wireless lamps, camping lanterns, oil lamps, or gas lamps may be rejected because of battery or hazardous material restrictions.

For customs, the item description should be clear. A good description would be:

Used table lamp without bulb and battery – Personal use

Avoid vague descriptions like “stuff,” “decoration,” “light,” or “electric item,” because German customs may ask for more information.

The lamp should be packed very carefully because it can break easily. Wrap the lamp body with several layers of bubble wrap, fill empty spaces inside the box, and use double-boxing if possible. The package should not move or make noise when shaken.

On the outside of the box, it is helpful to write:

FRAGILE
USED TABLE LAMP
NO BATTERY
NO BULB
PERSONAL USE

At the Korean post office, you can say:

“I want to send this to Germany by EMS. It is a regular plug-in table lamp. There is no battery inside, and the bulb has been removed. It is for personal use.”

After sending it, Korea Post will give a tracking number. The package will go from Korea to Germany, then go through German customs, and finally be delivered by the local postal/delivery service. German customs may ask for taxes or additional information depending on the declared value and item details.

The most important points are: remove the bulb, make sure there is no battery, pack it very safely, and write a clear customs description.
