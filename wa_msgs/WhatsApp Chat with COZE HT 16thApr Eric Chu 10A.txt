4/16/26, 1:25 PM - COZE_Genie: Why "cool" and "warm" are different worlds
two completely different systems
❄️ Air conditioning per room (individual air conditioner) ·Each bedroom and living room has a wall/ceiling separation air conditioner
·Use the remote control in each room (❄️Look for the button on the snow mark)
·No large ducts or central thermostat. I'm going to cool down only the room I'm in
🔥 Ondol floor heating: radiant underfloor heating
·A gas/electric boiler (often referred to as 보일러) circulates hot water under the floor or heats the floor with electricity
·The warmth spreads from your feet to your feet, so it's perfect for sitting on the floor, eating, and sleeping on the floor
·Manage from wall control panel (e.g. 난방=heating, 예약=timer, etc.)
In other words, the image of a "cooling machine that cools easily" and a "heating machine that warms the floor gently" living together in the same house.
Differences from American and European houses
🎛️ Many operations 1 vs thermostat
·Western houses often manage heating and cooling in one set of temperature settings
·In Korea, each room uses a different air conditioner remote control + floor heating wall panel
🌬️ Duct type vs. No ducts
·The HVAC, which is forced to blow, sends wind throughout the house to cool and cool
·Apartment houses in Korea have few ducts, cold air radiates directly from indoor units, and warm air radiates slowly from the floor (concrete)
⏱️ sensory speed
·Central air conditioning: warm/cold air coming relatively quickly
·Korea: Air conditioning is fast, but floor heating takes about 30-60 minutes to warm up. After that, even if you stop it, it will remain warm for a long time
🧦 quality of warmth
·Hot air hits the body in Western heating
·Ondol is warm on the floor itself, and it is warm as if it were wrapped from under your feet
💡 Energy saving zoning
·Some systems tend to heat and cool the whole house in one sitting
·In Korea, it is easy to reduce utility costs because it cools only the room you use and warms the floor only in the areas you need
Travelers Quick Tips
🔍 Find two operating systems first
·The remote control of the air conditioner is often near the indoor unit
·FLOOR HEATING PANELS ARE TENDLY INSTALLED IN ENTRANCE AND NEAR KITCHEN
⏰ Turn on the floor heating early
·If you put it in about an hour before going to bed, the floor will warm up well
🚪 Air conditioning improves efficiency by closing doors
·Corridors and bathrooms often have no air conditioning, so keep cold air in the room where you need it
🌦️ Can be used together during the change of seasons
·Dehumidification in one room and floor heating in another place are normal. No interference
🤳 take a picture of a panel
·The display varies from manufacturer to manufacturer, so if you have a picture, the host can show you right away
Why does Korea like this separation type?
🏢 Ideal for high-rise housing: Underfloor piping is compatible with concrete structure and requires no ducting
